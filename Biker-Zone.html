Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Replace your helmet if it's over 5-7 years old, involved in a crash, shows visible damage, has faded, or if the interior padding is deteriorating.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Inspect the tires, brakes, and overall condition of the bike; if everything looks good, perform a test ride in a safe area to ensure proper functionality before regular use. Consider a professional bike checkup if it has been in storage for an extended period.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Follow traffic laws, wear a helmet, use proper hand signals, stay visible with lights/reflectors, and be aware of your surroundings to ensure safe and responsible bike riding.
